Rakiura Track - Stewart Island

Exchange the hustle and bustle of mainland life for the tranquillity of the Rakiura Track on Stewart Island/Rakiura. Revel in the peacefulness with the bush, birds and beach at your side.

 

(other tips - accessed from icon/visual cue)

32 km / 19.9 mi

3 days

Don’t miss

  1. Glorious isolation is only a short boat ride or even shorter flight away. This is the ultimate island wilderness experience and a taste of the way New Zealand used to be. You’ll be welcomed by a cacophony of native birdsong and a warm greeting from the locals in the tiny settlement of Oban.
  2. From this year-round accessible track, you can enjoy exhilarating cliff-top walking between Lee Bay and Port William with fabulous vistas of the island’s gorgeous golden beaches and rugged coastline, and the protected bays of Paterson Inlet.
  3. Into history? Then you’ll find plenty here from early Maori kaika sites around Maori beach and Port William and the abandoned Shetland Islanders’ settlement to old log haulers and other relics from the sawmilling days dispersed along the track.
  4. The amazing Stewart Island Rakiura Tokoeka/Kiwi (unlike their cousins further north) can sometimes be seen foraging during daylight hours near the North Arm hut and campsite and can be heard calling at day’s end.
  5. Ulva Island, a predator-free island sanctuary, is just a short 5 minute water taxi ride from Oban, the island’s only township. Explore this island paradise, home to many rare and endangered native birds.

Before you go

It is important to plan and prepare so that you are well equipped to enjoy your Great Walk.

Before you go, know The Outdoor Safety Code - 5 simple rules to help you stay safe:

  1. Plan your trip
  2. Tell someone
  3. Be aware of the weather
  4. Know your limits
  5. Take sufficient supplies

Rakiura Track safety

  1. The weather is very changeable and difficult to predict. Walkers should come well-equipped and be prepared for all weather conditions, extreme weather can occur at any time of the year.

  2. Cell phone coverage is limited on the track. Telecom provides the only cell phone coverage on Stewart Island/Rakiura.

Things to know before you go

  1. A good level of fitness and the right outdoor clothing and equipment will greatly improve the enjoyment of your trip.

  2. Safety is your responsibility - leave your trip details with a trusted contact. Intention forms are available at adventuresmart.org.nz.

  3. Book your trip well in advance including your accommodation, transport and transfers to the start/end of the Great Walk.

  4. Pack all necessary equipment, your own food and drinks and any personal items for your trip including an extra day's supply of food and water.

  5. For a full list of equipment and preparation required for this walk, plus track updates for the Rakiura Track, please visit the DOC website

When to go

The Rakiura Track is suitable for walking all year round. During the winter months the weather on Stewart Island/Rakiura is generally cool, yet settled, making this track a great opportunity to relax, unwind and immerse yourself in idyllic surroundings any time of the year.

What to bring 

Your basic equipment needs to include

  1. Backpack
  2. Sleeping bag and liner
  3. Portable cooking stove
  4. Cooking utensils
  5. Food, incl. high-energy food
  6. Waterproof rain jacket and overtrousers
  7. Warm/thermal clothing
  8. Hiking boots
  9. Map and compass
  10. Lighter and matches
  11. Drink bottle & water

Add camping gear to your list if you’re staying at a campsite.

Travelling with children

The Rakiura Track is an ideal walk for families and suitable for anyone with moderate fitness. It takes 3 days to walk the 32 km circuit.

Getting there

Nearest airport

Invercargill - 65 km / 40 miles - to get to Stewart Island/Rakiura you can either choose to catch a 20 minute flight from Invercargill or you can drive from Invercargill to Bluff (20 minutes) and then catch a 1 hour ferry to the island’s main township, Oban.

Flights to this airport typically connect from Auckland, Christchurch and Wellington. The above driving times are indicative only - always allow for extra time to get to your destination.

How to get to the trailhead

Rakiura Track is situated on New Zealand's Stewart Island/Rakiura, just a 20 minute flight from Invercargill or an hour by ferry from Bluff. The track begins at Lee Bay, the official entrance to Rakiura National Park.

Where to stay

DOC provides two huts and three campsites along the Rakiura Track.

Huts

The Rakiura Track huts provide heating, toilets, bunks, mattresses and cold running water. Note: huts do not provide pots and pans, cooking utensils, hot showers, linen, toilet paper or food and drinks for purchase. Make sure you put these things on your checklist before you go.

  1. Port William Hut: 24 bunks
  2. North Arm Hut: 24 bunks


Campsites

Each hut has a camping site nearby and there is an additional campsite at Maori Beach. Campsites offer basic facilities including a water supply, cooking shelter and toilets. Campers are not permitted to use the hut facilities.

Cost and booking

Fees for huts and campsites are charged per person, per night.

Bookings are essential! Use the DOC online booking system prior to travel or call 0800 NZ GREATWALKS (0800 694 732).

On the track

Track description

The Rakiura Track is a 32 km circuit and can be walked in either direction. It can comfortably be walked in three days for anyone with a moderate level of fitness. Shuttle services are available from the island’s township of Oban to the start / end of the track.

Halfmoon Bay to Port William Hut

4-5 hours, 12 km / 7 mi

Step through the chain link sculpture commissioned to recognise the forming of the Rakiura National Park, to start your island adventure. Enjoy stunning coastal views as the track makes its way from Lee Bay to picturesque Little River, which is crossed by bridge. Rounding the tip of Peters Point brings you to Maori Beach, once home to several sawmills and a school. An old boiler is all that remains of a once thriving sawmilling community. From here it is a sweeping beach walk to the swing bridge before climbing up the forested ridge and dropping down into Magnetic Beach and Port William Hut at the far end.

Port William Hut to North Arm Hut

6 hours, 13 km / 8 mi

Make your way through regenerating podocarp forest and then dense bush as you leave the east coast behind you. Take a breather at the old log haulers that used to drag the forest giants out of the gullies and hillsides, travel down the tramlines which both show remnants of a bygone world, and reflect on the hardy settlers who worked the saws to retain a livelihood.

North Arm Hut to Halfmoon Bay

4-5 hours, 12 km / 7 mi

From here it’s a moderate and undulating walk in the forest fringe consisting of kāmahi and rimu surrounded by stunning views across Paterson Inlet/Whaka a Te Wera. Follow the coast down to secluded bays, watch the birds wading at low tide in Sawdust Bay and then on to Kaipipi Bay where a small grassy knoll on the water’s edge allows a great perspective over the sheltered waterway.
